Your Current Stack
Brazil Nuts2-3 daily
Selenium supplementation - directly reduces thyroid antibody levels (TPO and TG antibodies). One of the most evidence-backed interventions for Hashimoto's. Do not exceed 4 per day to avoid toxicity.
Timing: Morning with food - Non-negotiable
Pumpkin SeedsDaily handful
Zinc for thyroid function - required for T4 to T3 conversion and immune regulation. Also supports testosterone maintenance during high training load.
Timing: Anytime - good as a training snack
Aloe VeraDaily - inner leaf gel
Gut lining support. Hashimoto's is strongly linked to intestinal permeability. Aloe vera soothes and repairs gut lining, reduces systemic inflammation that drives thyroid antibody production.
Timing: Morning on empty stomach
Magnesium Glycinate300-400mg before bed
Critical for thyroid function - required cofactor for T4 to T3 conversion. Also dramatically improves sleep quality, reduces muscle soreness, and supports nervous system recovery. Glycinate form has superior absorption.
Timing: 30-60 min before bed - non-negotiable
Creatine Monohydrate5g daily
Muscle maintenance during concurrent endurance training. Preserves strength and power output as cardio volume increases. Also has cognitive benefits useful given brain fog associated with Hashimoto's.
Timing: Post-workout - consistency matters more than timing
Gluten-Free DietNON-NEGOTIABLE
Primary Hashimoto's intervention. Your TSH dropped from 6.8 to 3.5 after going gluten-free. Gluten molecular structure closely resembles thyroid tissue - in people with HT, gluten consumption triggers an immune response that attacks the thyroid. No exceptions. Ever.
Every meal - every day - no exceptions
Recommended Additions
Vitamin D3 + K2
Vitamin D deficiency is nearly universal in Hashimoto's and directly worsens thyroid antibody levels. Optimal level for Hashimoto's athletes is 60-80 ng/mL. K2 is essential alongside D3. Get your level tested first. Typical dose: 4000-6000 IU D3 + 100-200mcg K2 MK-7. Priority supplement - add immediately.
Omega-3 Fish Oil - 2-3g EPA/DHA
Systemic inflammation reduction. EPA/DHA directly reduce inflammatory cytokines and support cardiovascular adaptation to endurance training. Use high-quality triglyceride form - not ethyl ester. Take with food.
Vitamin B12 - Methylcobalamin form
B12 deficiency is extremely common in Hashimoto's. Symptoms overlap with hypothyroidism: fatigue, brain fog, poor recovery. Use methylcobalamin - the active form your body actually uses. Get levels tested.
Iron (with Vitamin C)
Iron deficiency impairs thyroid hormone synthesis and is common in endurance athletes with autoimmune conditions. Test serum ferritin before supplementing - ferritin below 40 ng/mL in an endurance athlete warrants attention. Take with vitamin C for absorption.
These are nutritional support recommendations based on research and your specific Hashimoto's profile - not medical advice. Always confirm supplement additions with your endocrinologist, particularly anything that could interact with thyroid medication or affect thyroid hormone levels.
