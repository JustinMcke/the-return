Supplements
Five supplements with the strongest evidence base for strength and endurance athletes. No fluff — just what actually moves the needle.
Creatine Monohydrate 5g daily
The most researched supplement in existence. Increases phosphocreatine stores for ATP resynthesis — more power, more reps, faster recovery between hard efforts. Works for both strength and endurance athletes. Also has meaningful cognitive benefits. Monohydrate is the only form worth buying — ignore the marketing on everything else.
Timing: Any time — consistency matters far more than timing. Post-workout if you want a ritual.
Vitamin D3 + K2 2000–4000 IU D3 · 100mcg K2
Vitamin D deficiency is endemic — most people are deficient year-round. Low D directly suppresses testosterone, impairs muscle protein synthesis, and tanks immune function. K2 (MK-7 form) directs calcium into bones rather than arteries. Always take together. Get your levels tested — optimal for athletes is 50–80 ng/mL.
Timing: Morning with food containing fat. D3 is fat-soluble — absorption drops significantly without fat present.
Magnesium Glycinate 300–400mg before bed
Magnesium is a cofactor in over 300 enzymatic reactions including muscle contraction, protein synthesis, and energy production. Athletes deplete it through sweat. Deficiency wrecks sleep quality, raises cortisol, and causes cramping. Glycinate form has superior absorption and won't cause the GI issues that oxide or citrate do.
Timing: 30–60 min before bed. Noticeably improves sleep quality within a week.
Omega-3 Fish Oil 2–3g EPA/DHA daily
EPA and DHA reduce systemic inflammation, support cardiovascular adaptation to training, and improve muscle protein synthesis. Also meaningfully improves mood and cognition. Use triglyceride form — not ethyl ester — for 70% better absorption. Check your label: most bottles show total oil weight, not EPA/DHA content.
Timing: With meals containing fat. Prevents fishy aftertaste and improves absorption.
Ashwagandha KSM-66 600mg daily
Adaptogen with genuine clinical evidence — not the usual herbal hand-waving. KSM-66 extract reduces cortisol, improves testosterone in men under training stress, increases VO2 max in endurance athletes, and improves sleep quality. Particularly useful during high-volume training blocks. Use KSM-66 specifically — it's the extract used in the studies.
Timing: With food. Some take at night for sleep benefits — either works.
General recommendations based on published research — not medical advice. Consult your doctor before starting any supplement, especially if you're on medication or managing a health condition.
